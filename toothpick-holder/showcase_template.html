How it works

Chuck a pick like a bit

The tapered nose ends in a Ø 2.4 mm socket. Push a round toothpick in and its natural taper wedges tight — held straight and firm, swapped in a second.

Magazine in the grip

The handle is hollow: a Ø 14.6 mm bore swallows about twenty standard 65 mm toothpicks, so the refill lives in your pocket with you.

Screws shut, stays shut

A scallop-knurled cap threads into the back of the handle on a coarse 2.5 mm trapezoid thread — printable at 0.2 mm layers, sealed in three easy turns.
